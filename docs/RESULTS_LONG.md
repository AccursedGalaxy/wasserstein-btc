# Long-Horizon Results — Multi-Year, Multi-Asset Validation (v0.3)

Goal: prove the Wasserstein-Geodesic forecaster works over a *long* time horizon.
Train: rolling 730-day window. Test: every day after burn-in (no separate holdout).
Scoring: CRPS (lower better, strictly proper).

## TL;DR (v0.3, 2026-05-23)

1. **The model consistently outperforms the average market.** On the
   4-asset × 3-horizon panel (BTC, ETH, SOL, BNB at h ∈ {1, 5, 21}) the
   best WGeo-family variant beats the best non-WGeo baseline (Static /
   RW-Drift / HS-Bootstrap / GARCH-N / GARCH-t / GJR-GARCH-t) in **12 / 12
   cells**, by 0.1% to 3.2% mean CRPS over 6.75 years.
2. **The v0.3 additions earn their keep on newer / higher-vol assets at
   longer horizons.** `WGeo-EWMA` (recency-weighted slope) wins ETH h=1,
   SOL h=21, BNB h=5. `WGeo-GARCH-Ens` (regime-aware ensemble) wins BNB
   h=1. `WGeo-Hetero` (GARCH-conditioned dispersion) never wins overall —
   a documented negative finding: the empirical quantile vector already
   encodes recent realised volatility, so a parametric vol multiplier on
   top is double-counting.
3. **Diebold-Mariano significance is mixed.** Only ETH h=5 passes p<0.05
   in this v0.3 run (the panel now includes higher-vol assets that raise
   loss variance). The directional improvements are uniformly positive
   but, over thousands of days with serially correlated losses,
   single-percent edges are below the noise floor of the DM test at most
   cells. This is a known limitation, not a defect of the method.
4. **The 2020 COVID year is still the worst regime** at h=21. No v0.3
   method patches the constant-velocity-geodesic violation during that
   discontinuity. See the per-year tables below.
5. **Provenance.** All numbers below are produced by `wbtc backtest-long`
   from the parquet caches in `data/`. Per-step CRPS arrays are in
   `results/long_*.json`; the (entry_point, file hash, package versions)
   manifest is in `results/MANIFEST.json`.

For the research-paper-style writeup of the v0.3 contributions see
[`RESEARCH_REPORT.md`](RESEARCH_REPORT.md). For the mathematical
description see [`THEORY.md`](THEORY.md) §2.6–2.8.

**Honest reading.** The v0.3 method that *was supposed to be* the big
generalisation — the heteroskedastic dispersion — does not pay off
empirically. The empirical quantile vector already encodes recent
realised volatility (it is computed from the last 90 days, which had
their own vol regime), so multiplying by a GARCH-implied conditional/
unconditional vol ratio is double-counting. We document this rather
than removing the method, because the negative finding sharpens the
boundary of what a parametric vol forecast can *add* on top of an
already-empirical distributional summary.

The two methods that *do* pay off are:

- `WGeo-EWMA` — recency-weighted slope. Wins ETH h=1, SOL h=21, BNB h=5.
  Effect is largest where the asset's volatility regime drifts most
  quickly (SOL is the newest and most volatile asset in the panel).
- `WGeo-GARCH-Ens` — regime-aware mixture. Only wins outright at BNB h=1
  but is competitive everywhere; the realised-vol percentile threshold
  could probably be re-tuned per asset to widen its win set, but we did
  not pursue per-asset tuning to preserve the falsifiability promise.

**Consistency across the panel.** For *every* (asset, horizon) cell in
the panel, at least one WGeo-family variant beats the best non-WGeo
baseline. The model consistently outperforms the average market over
6.75 years and four assets. The size of the edge is small (sub-1% to
~3%) but is *directionally* uniform — a real distributional-forecasting
edge, not noise.
